Our Clients

Built for Buildings Where Conditions Matter

We specialize in facilities where temperature, humidity, air pressure, and air quality directly affect operations, occupant safety, and regulatory compliance.

Who We Work With

Three Types of Clients. One Solution.

🏥

Medical & Surgical Facilities

Eye surgery centers, ambulatory surgery centers, medical offices, and specialized clinics where environmental control is critical to patient safety and regulatory compliance.

Common Pain Points

  • Staff has no way to verify room pressures are correct
  • HVAC alarms go unnoticed until a complaint is filed
  • No documentation of ongoing HVAC performance
  • Accreditation surveys catch issues that were long-standing

What We Solve

  • Weekly verification of OR and procedure room pressures
  • Air changes per hour (ACH) confirmation
  • Automated alarm monitoring and reporting
  • Documented performance history for inspections
🏢

Property Management Companies

Companies managing multiple commercial buildings, office complexes, or mixed-use properties where HVAC maintenance across a portfolio is difficult to track consistently.

Common Pain Points

  • Can't be at every building to check on HVAC daily
  • Tenant complaints are the first sign of a problem
  • Maintenance contractors respond, not prevent
  • No visibility into equipment health between service visits

What We Solve

  • Weekly multi-building health summary in one report
  • Catch issues before tenants call with complaints
  • Identify energy waste driving up utility bills
  • Prioritize which buildings need attention first
🔧

Building Owners & Facility Managers

Individual building owners and in-house facility managers who want proactive HVAC oversight without adding headcount or relying solely on reactive service calls.

Common Pain Points

  • HVAC problems only surface when something breaks
  • Hard to justify preventive maintenance budget
  • No data to back up maintenance decisions to ownership
  • Emergency repair costs are unpredictable and high

What We Solve

  • Weekly proactive identification of developing issues
  • Hard data to support maintenance budget decisions
  • Extend equipment lifespan through early intervention
  • Dramatically reduce emergency repair frequency
Medical Specialty Focus

Why Medical Facilities Are Our Most Critical Clients

In a commercial office building, an HVAC failure means discomfort and complaints. In an operating room or sterile procedure suite, the same failure can mean a patient safety incident, a failed inspection, or a canceled procedure schedule.

Medical facilities operate under strict environmental standards from ASHRAE, The Joint Commission, and state health departments. Meeting these standards requires ongoing verification — not just a one-time commissioning check when the building was built.

We understand the difference between a negative pressure isolation room and a positive pressure OR suite. We know what air change rates matter for which spaces, and we know how to document that your systems are performing as required — week after week.
